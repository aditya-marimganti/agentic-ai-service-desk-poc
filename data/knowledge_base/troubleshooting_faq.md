# General Troubleshooting FAQ

**Printer issues:** If a printer is not responding, first check that it shows as "online" in your device's printer settings. Most printer issues are resolved by removing and re-adding the printer using the internal printer setup tool. If the issue persists, it may be a network-level fault and should be reported to IT with the printer's location and asset tag number.

**Email issues:** If you are not receiving emails, first check your spam/junk folder and confirm your mailbox is not over its storage quota (visible in your email settings). Most delivery delays are temporary and resolve within 30 minutes. If email has been down for longer than that, check the internal status page before submitting a ticket, since this may be a known outage.

**WiFi issues:** For unstable WiFi connections in the office, ensure you are connected to the "Company-Secure" network and not a guest network, as guest networks have limited bandwidth and are not intended for daily work use. Restarting your device's WiFi adapter typically resolves minor connectivity drops.

**General rule:** For any issue not covered here or in other IT documentation, or if a suggested fix does not resolve the problem, submit a ticket through the Service Portal rather than continuing to troubleshoot independently, so it can be properly tracked and escalated if needed.
